The problem

Most candidates walk in undertrained, not underqualified.

Skill isn't usually the gap. Practice is. Strong candidates lose offers to nerves, rambling answers, and pacing that falls apart the moment a question gets personal — not because they lack the experience to back it up.

Real mock interviews need a mentor, a trainer, or a generous friend with free time. That makes consistent practice expensive, hard to schedule, and dependent on whoever happens to be available — so most people simply skip it.

01 / ACCESS

Mentors don't scale

One trainer can only run so many mock sessions a week, for so many students.

02 / COST

Coaching is expensive

Quality interview coaching is priced for the few, not for every graduating class.

03 / CONSISTENCY

Feedback is uneven

Two mentors rarely agree on what "good" sounded like — so the feedback contradicts itself.

The solution

One coach, available every time you need to rehearse.

AI Interview Mirror runs the loop a great mentor would — ask, listen, evaluate, reflect — except it's ready at 11pm before the interview, not just during office hours.

STEP 01

Ask role-specific questions

Tell it the role, the industry, even the company you're targeting — the interview adapts its questions to match.

STEP 02

Listen and evaluate

Every answer is read for clarity, confidence, pacing, and how well it actually addresses the question asked.

STEP 03

Reflect specific feedback

You get a session report — exact strengths, exact weak spots, and what to change before the next attempt.
